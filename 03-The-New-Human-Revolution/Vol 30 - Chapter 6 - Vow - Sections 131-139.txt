# Volume 30: Chapter 6, Vows 131–139

## Vow 131
Shin'ichi Yamamoto addressed SGI representatives from Singapore and Australia, discussing the Buddhist significance of the lion as representing the mentor-disciple relationship. He emphasized that courage is essential for practitioners, stating that "we all possess courage" and urged members to develop "the heart of a lion king" to overcome weakness and achieve victory in life.

## Vow 132
Shin'ichi visited Malaysia for the first time in 12 years, witnessing significant growth in both the country and the Soka Gakkai Malaysia organization. He received an honorary doctorate from Universiti Putra Malaysia, emphasizing in his acceptance speech that "genuine dialogue, rooted in sincere friendship" can overcome divisions and promote peace through education.

## Vow 133
The honorary doctorate from a Malaysian university—a Muslim-majority nation—represented interfaith recognition and mutual understanding. Shin'ichi later received additional honors from other Malaysian universities, and he met with Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad to discuss youth development and humanity's future.

## Vow 134
In Hong Kong for his 20th visit, Shin'ichi reflected on pioneering member Chow Chi Kong's tireless letter-writing efforts to encourage scattered members across Asia. He stressed that "home visits and personal guidance" circulate "the lifeblood of faith and human warmth" throughout the organization.

## Vow 135
Shin'ichi highlighted Hong Kong's significance in the westward transmission of Buddhism and the Soka Gakkai's international expansion, including the first overseas Soka kindergarten. He attended a dinner at Hong Kong Chief Secretary Anson Chan's official residence, reflecting on dialogue between different cultures and religions.

## Vow 136
Shin'ichi received an honorary doctorate from the Chinese University of Hong Kong, becoming the first Japanese recipient of such an honor from that institution. He then returned to Japan, concluding his 20th-century travels in Osaka to begin the new millennium with Kansai members.

## Vow 137
In 2001, designated the "Year of Total Victory for the New Century," Shin'ichi contributed a New Year's poem and launched Soka University of America in California. Following the September 11 terrorist attacks, he called for dialogue and interfaith cooperation, stating that terrorism is "absolutely unacceptable" regardless of perceived justifications.

## Vow 138
After 9/11, Shin'ichi urged global citizens to unite for peace through dialogue and humanitarian education. He emphasized that lasting peace requires addressing "human rights, eradication of poverty, and disarmament," and that the Soka Gakkai's mission centers on human revolution through education promoting "humane values."

## Vow 139
Speaking to youth division members, Shin'ichi expressed determination to pass the mentor-disciple mission to the next generation, stating: "The development of kosen-rufu depends on the existence of genuine disciples!" He envisioned youth as "Bodhisattvas of the Earth" advancing the organization's global peace mission.
