## Overview

This section from *The New Human Revolution* documents Shin'ichi Yamamoto's visits to Shikoku in November 1981, focusing on his encouragement of members and his collaborative creation of the "Song of Crimson" with young men's division leaders.

## Key Sections

**Cheers of Victory 11-12:** Shin'ichi attends a Tokushima auditorium completion ceremony where a women's chorus performs. He emphasizes that embracing the Mystic Law provides dynamic life force to overcome difficulties. Later, youth leader Okimitsu Owada requests a meeting with Shikoku youth representatives, which Shin'ichi readily accepts.

**Cheers of Victory 13-14:** An exhibition organized by Shikoku youth showcasing Shin'ichi's peace activities draws over 61,000 visitors. At a Kagawa leaders meeting, Shin'ichi declares his renewed leadership, stating "I will take the lead once again!" He visits construction sites and auditoriums throughout Kagawa, emphasizing Shikoku's role as a "forerunner" in kosen-rufu.

**Cheers of Victory 15-18:** Youth representatives work overnight to compose lyrics for a new song. Shin'ichi provides extensive guidance, discussing the importance of fresh, original expressions. He revises approximately 30 phrases, emphasizing that the song should honor pioneer members who built the Soka Gakkai despite hardship and prejudice.

**Cheers of Victory 19-20:** At a Kochi Chapter anniversary gongyo session, Shin'ichi teaches that "times of trouble reveal the essence of a person's faith." He continues refining the song's lyrics while attending various meetings, demonstrating his commitment to creating something enduring through persistent effort and creative struggle.

## Central Themes

- The mentor-disciple relationship as essential to kosen-rufu advancement
- Youth as agents of innovation and organizational renewal
- Gratitude toward pioneer members whose sacrifices enabled later success
- The value of persistent refinement in creative work
- Faith as demonstrated through courageous action during adversity
