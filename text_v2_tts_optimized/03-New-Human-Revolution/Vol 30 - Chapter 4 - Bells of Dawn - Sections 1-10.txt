## Overview

This chapter documents Shin'ichi Yamamoto's 1981 visit to Europe, beginning in Germany and extending to Bulgaria. The narrative explores themes of religious reformation, international dialogue, and the spread of Nichiren Buddhism.

## Key Sections

**Bells of Dawn 1-2**: Yamamoto arrives in Frankfurt on May 16, 1981, comparing Martin Luther's 16th-century reformation to modern Buddhist practice. He emphasizes the importance of dialogues with academic leaders across various disciplines.

**Bells of Dawn 3-4**: A friendship gathering celebrates the 20th anniversary of Buddhism's development in Germany, with 800 members from eight countries attending. Yamamoto addresses the crowd, stating that "we have a right to become happy here on earth" and that Nichiren Buddhism provides the foundation for genuine peace and human fulfillment.

**Bells of Dawn 5-7**: Yamamoto discusses human revolution as essential to solving global problems, addresses divorce as a private matter requiring respect and confidentiality from leaders, and explains the organizational structure of the Soka Gakkai as necessary for supporting individual spiritual development.

**Bells of Dawn 8**: Yamamoto visits Goethe's house in Frankfurt, reflecting on how great writers inspire hope and determine to continue his life's work building foundations for global Buddhist practice.

**Bells of Dawn 9-10**: Yamamoto travels to Bulgaria, meeting with government officials and visiting Sofia University, where he receives an honorary doctorate in education and sociology.
