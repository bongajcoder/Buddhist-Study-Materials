In the “Supernatural Powers” chapter,
Shakyamuni Buddha transfers the essence of
the Lotus Sutra’ to Bodhisattva Superior Prac-
tices and the other Bodhisattvas of the Earth,
He then outlines how these bodhisattvas
should exert themselves in propagating the
sutra after his passing. The above passage
cited by Nichiren at the end of “On Attaining
Buddhahood in This Lifetime” is the conclud-
ing part of Shakyamuni’s description of
benefits to be gained as a result of those
efforts. Here, “[one] should accept and
uphold this sutra” means accepting and
upholding the daimoku of the Lotus Sutra,
Myoho-renge-kyo—the essence of the teach-
ing transmitted by the Buddha to Bodhisattva
Superior Practices.
